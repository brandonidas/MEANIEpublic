The Fallacy of Choice

People are both social and intellectual beings, this has allowed humanity intellectual collaboration that has pushed us forward into the future.

However, such a combination of two, sometimes contradictory, traits produces unwanted confusion. This is when a person's intellect fails because a choice is inherently social but attempts to resolve it anyway.

Take for example, the straight A student, able to navigate the complex nuances of her academic work but unable to choose her outfit for the date.

This is because as intelligent as one may be, when confronted with a social choice, human beings need social evidence to make that choice.

Certain social choices are easy to make but others are not and depending on the state of mind one is in, even a mundane social choice becomes excruciating, even for people who deem themselves self-directed and decisive.

Choiciture Works To Make Collecting Social Evidence Easy

Through the use of simple social media concepts and UX design, we've created an application that makes it easy to collect social sentiment for your social choices.

Although, as at February 2015, it's still in it's prototype phase, it's core functionality complete and is usable right now with a full Twitter-like social follower system and functionality for posting of "Choices".

Choices are which are simple collections of 1-4 images used as "options" to be liked as a means of social evidence, a long with a caption to be used as a question, to add some context. These are posts that users can create to acquire social evidence from their followers. (example below)
